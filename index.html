Featured Research

Innovating cost-effective protocols in animal biotechnology.

Undergraduate Thesis Research: A Cost-Effective Papain-Based Cardiomyocyte Tissue Dissociation Protocol for Banaba Philippine Native Chicken Embryos

Advisor: Percival P. Sangel, DVM, Ph.D.

This study addressed a critical gap in avian-specific cell culture by developing and validating a cost-effective protocol for isolating heart muscle cells (cardiomyocytes) from native chicken embryos. The research aimed to find a more efficient and economical alternative to traditional enzymes like trypsin.

The results demonstrated that papain, a plant-derived enzyme, is a superior agent for this application. It yielded significantly higher initial cell viability and was the most economical option, paving the way for more accessible and scalable research in avian cardiac biology and genetics.

Broader Impact

This research provides a foundational tool for advancements in:

  • Biomedical Research: Improving models for cardiac physiology and disease.
  • Animal Biotechnology: Facilitating genetic engineering and trait enhancement in poultry.
  • Conservation Biology: Aiding genetic conservation of indigenous breeds.
